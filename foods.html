Hong Kong Foods

Center Image

1. Dim Sum- Bite-sized portions of dumplings, buns, and other snacks, typically served in small bamboo steamers.

2. Char Siu- Sweet and savory barbecued pork, often served with rice or noodles.

3. Wonton Noodles- A noodle dish featuring thin egg noodles topped with wontons (dumplings filled with shrimp or pork) and broth.

4. Egg Tarts- A pastry shell filled with a smooth, sweet egg custard, similar to a small tart.

5. Pineapple Bun- A soft, sweet bun with a crispy, sugary topping resembling the skin of a pineapple.

6. Beef Brisket Noodles- Tender beef brisket cooked in a savory broth, served with noodles and often vegetables.

7. Hong Kong-style Milk Tea- Strong black tea mixed with evaporated or condensed milk, served hot or cold.

8. Roast Goose- Crispy-skinned and tender roast goose, often served with rice and a flavorful gravy.

9. Cheung Fun- Rice noodles rolled into thin sheets, filled with savory ingredients like shrimp, beef, or pork, then drizzled with soy sauce.

10. Siu Mai- Open-topped dumplings, typically filled with pork and shrimp, served in bamboo steamers.

11. Chow Mein- Stir-fried noodles with a mix of vegetables, meat, or seafood, often served with a savory sauce.

12. Congee- A rice porridge often served with a variety of toppings like pickled vegetables, meats, or century eggs.

13. Stinky Tofu- Fermented tofu, deep-fried to a crispy texture, and typically served with chili sauce.

14. Claypot Rice- Rice cooked in a clay pot with a variety of toppings like chicken, Chinese sausage, or pork, often accompanied by soy sauce.

15. Hong Kong-style French Toast- Thick slices of bread dipped in egg batter, fried, and served with butter and syrup, often with peanut butter or jam.

16. Egg Waffles (Gai Daan Jai)- A sweet, egg-based batter cooked into a puffed, waffle-like shape, often served hot and crispy.

17. Fish Balls- Chewy, round fish meatballs often served in soup or with skewers, flavored with a savory broth or sauce.

18. Lai Char (Cantonese Style Tea)- Tea with a mix of milk and sugar, often enjoyed with meals, especially dim sum.

19. Hong Kong-style Porridge (Juk)- A savory, thick rice porridge often made with chicken, pork, or fish, commonly eaten for breakfast.

20. Mango Pomelo Sago- A refreshing dessert made with fresh mango, pomelo, and sago pearls, often served cold with coconut milk.
