Hong Kong Travel Guide

VISA Requirements

Center Image

1. As a Philippine passport holder, you can visit Hong Kong without a visa for tourism or business purposes for up to 14 days.

2. For longer stays or other activities such as employment or study, you will need to apply for the appropriate visa or entry permit before traveling.

3. Ensure your passport is valid for at least one month beyond your intended stay in Hong Kong.

4. Tourists: Many countries’ citizens can enter Hong Kong without a visa for stays of up to 7, 14, or 90 days, depending on nationality.

5. Hong Kong offers working holiday schemes for citizens from certain countries, allowing them to work and travel.

6. Immigration Form: You'll likely need to complete an arrival card, which can sometimes be done digitally. Customs Declarations: If you’re carrying items above the duty-free limit or restricted goods, you may need to declare them at customs.

7. For stays longer than the visa-free period, the tourist visa must be applied for in advance at the nearest Hong Kong consulate or via online channels.

8. It’s highly recommended, though not mandatory, for travelers to have travel insurance that covers COVID-19 and medical emergencies.

9. Foreigners intending to take up paid employment in Hong Kong must apply for this visa, with a sponsoring employer.

10. Students planning to study in Hong Kong for more than 6 months need to apply for a student visa.

11. Hong Kong’s currency is the Hong Kong Dollar (HKD). Credit cards are widely accepted, but it's a good idea to have some cash on hand.

12. Hong Kong generally has strict public health protocols on public transport, so you may need to wear masks or adhere to any other regulations on transit.

13. Hong Kong has strict laws around certain items like drugs, weapons, and gambling.

14. Always familiarize yourself with local customs and regulations to avoid any issues.

15. Having an emergency contact in Hong Kong or registering with your embassy (if applicable) could be a good idea for safety.

Travel Requirements

16. Ensure your passport is valid for at least one month beyond your intended stay in Hong Kong.

17. Proof of Sufficient Funds: Immigration authorities may ask for proof that you can financially support your stay. Bank statements or proof of sponsorship (if applicable) may be required.

18. You may be asked to provide the address where you'll be staying in Hong Kong, such as a hotel reservation or a letter from your host if you're staying with someone.

19. Evidence of a return flight or a flight to your next destination.

20. As of now, Hong Kong does not require specific vaccinations for entry.

21. Certain items are prohibited or restricted in Hong Kong, such as narcotics, counterfeit goods, endangered species, and items that violate intellectual property laws.

22. Extensions are granted based on individual circumstances, and extensions for tourism are not guaranteed.

23. Be prepared to answer questions regarding the purpose of your visit, your travel plans, and whether you intend to stay beyond the permitted time.

24. If you are a permanent resident or have long-term residency in Hong Kong, you may have special permits or an ID card for easier entry.

25. Hong Kong is one of the world’s leading financial centers, ranking alongside New York and London.

26. Ensure you carry the necessary documents.

27. If you're from one of the eligible countries, you can apply for a work-and-travel visa to stay in Hong Kong for up to one year.

28. Children or minors traveling to Hong Kong should have their own passport and may be asked to provide proof of relationship to the accompanying adult, such as a birth certificate.

29. Travelers must pass through customs, where they may be asked to declare items they are bringing into the country, including goods that exceed the duty-free limits or prohibited items.

30. If you’re traveling with pets, especially dogs and cats, you must meet Hong Kong’s pet import requirements.
